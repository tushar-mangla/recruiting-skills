# Role: Software Project Manager

## What Worked
- **Best channels**: LinkedIn Recruiter search with "Project Manager" AND "Agile" AND "SaaS" — 30% response rate on personalized InMails
- **Best outreach approach**: Reference specific projects they delivered (product launches, migrations, platform scaling). PMs respond well to being recognized for delivery outcomes, not just methodology.
- **Key communities**: PMI local chapters, Agile/Scrum meetup groups, r/projectmanagement, Startupjobs.cz
- **Effective screening signals**: Quantified delivery outcomes (e.g., "shipped X for Y users"), team size managed, experience with cross-functional teams, evidence of process improvement (not just following process)
- **Referrals**: Asking existing PMs in our network yielded 3 strong candidates — PMs know other PMs

## What Didn't Work
- Generic job board posts on Indeed — attracted mostly junior candidates or career-changers without relevant tech PM experience
- Overly technical language in outreach — PMs are not developers, writing about "our microservices architecture" turned off more than it attracted
- LinkedIn connection requests without a note — 5% acceptance rate vs 35% with a personalized note
- Posting in general tech Facebook groups — low signal, mostly irrelevant applications

## Notes
- PMs respond better to impact/autonomy talk ("you'll own delivery end-to-end") than to compensation-first messaging
- Typical notice period: 2-3 months in EU markets, 2 weeks in US
- Many strong PMs don't have PMP certification — don't filter them out automatically
- Scrum Masters with delivery ownership experience are often excellent PM candidates (adjacent title)
- In the Czech market, "Project Manager" often includes non-tech PM roles — need to specify "software" or "tech" in searches
